ENGLISH VERSION（Harvard-Style Explanation + Locke Framework）

**How Locke’s Theory of Property Explains the Controversy of Original Land Distribution:

South Africa vs. Taiwan**

John Locke argued that every human being possesses three fundamental natural rights: life, liberty, and property. Property, in Locke’s view, arises when a person mixes their labor with natural resources—land, fish, fruit, or anything originally unowned. However, Locke also imposed two essential conditions:
	1.	The land must truly be unowned (“vacant land”).
	2.	One must leave “enough and as good” for others (the Lockean proviso).

When these conditions are violated, property claims lose their legitimacy.

South Africa: When Colonial Land Claims Violated Locke’s Principles

In colonial South Africa, Europeans declared vast territories as “unowned” simply because African communities did not use European-style land deeds. This was philosophically false: African societies practiced communal land ownership, which still counts as ownership under Locke’s framework.

Moreover, the 1913 Natives Land Act restricted Black South Africans to only 7% of the land, leaving the overwhelming majority to white settlers. This directly violated Locke’s proviso—it did not leave enough resources for others, and it forcibly removed people from lands they traditionally lived on.

Thus, colonial land ownership in South Africa was not a realization of Locke’s theory but a distortion of it—using law and power to legitimize dispossession.

Taiwan: Land-to-the-Tiller as a Lockean-style Reform

Taiwan’s “Land to the Tiller” reform reflects the opposite trajectory. After the end of Japanese rule, the government redistributed land to the actual farmers who cultivated it. This aligned with Locke’s labor theory:
	•	Those who worked the land received its ownership.
	•	Former landlords received compensation to reduce conflict.

This reform established a more just property regime and laid the foundation for Taiwan’s rural stability and later economic development. It is frequently cited as a modern example of Lockean property justice.

Why Locke Leads to Limited Government

Locke believed that people leave the “state of nature” and form a limited government to prevent three dangers:
	1.	Someone may seize another’s land.
	2.	Someone may restrict another’s freedom.
	3.	Someone may threaten another’s life.

Because property is a natural right, the government exists to protect life, liberty, and estate—not to violate them. When a government becomes abusive or confiscatory, Locke argues that it loses legitimacy, and people regain their right of revolution.

South Africa shows what happens when government itself becomes the violator.
Taiwan shows what happens when government protects and restores property rights.

Lori Insight (English Version)

In Locke’s framework, the starting point of land distribution reveals the moral structure of a society. The core problem in South Africa was that colonial powers used law to define ownership and force to determine who could possess land—directly violating Locke’s two foundational principles. In contrast, Taiwan’s Land-to-the-Tiller reform returned land to those who actually cultivated it, realizing Locke’s idea that labor creates rightful ownership. This helps me understand why Locke insists on the necessity of limited government—only a government formed through the consent of the governed can avoid violating people’s natural rights to life, liberty, and property. Whether a land system is just or unjust often determines whether a political order develops toward freedom or toward authoritarianism.

中文版（哲學脈絡＋歷史比較）

**洛克財產權理論如何解釋土地分配爭議：

南非與台灣的對照**

洛克主張每個人天生都擁有三大自然權：生命、自由、財產。
財產之所以產生，是因為人類透過勞動混入自然物，使其成為自己的正當所有：
	•	荒地被耕作 → 可成為耕作者的土地
	•	捕魚者擁有魚
	•	採集者擁有果實

但洛克也設下兩個重要限制：
	1.	該土地或資源必須是真正的「無主地」。
	2.	必須「留下足夠而同樣好的資源給別人」（洛克限制條款）。

只要違反其一，財產主張就不具正當性。

南非：殖民土地制度如何違背洛克的核心原則

在南非，白人殖民者以「黑人沒有地契 → 土地是無主地」為理由，宣布大面積土地可由殖民政府「合法取得」。但這在洛克框架下毫無正當性：
	•	黑人採用的是部落共同土地制度，不是無主地。
	•	1913 年《黑人土地法》把 87% 土地劃為白人區，黑人只能擁有極小比例。
	•	這不僅不能稱為「留下足夠的資源」，反而是剝奪與壓迫。

換句話說，南非的殖民土地制度不是洛克思想的實踐，而是利用法律與武力包裝的掠奪。

台灣：耕者有其田是洛克式改革的典型案例

台灣在 1950 年代進行土地改革，把土地分配給真正耕作者，同時補償地主。這完全符合洛克的正義觀：
	•	透過勞動取得所有權
	•	政府介入防止少數人壟斷土地
	•	減少剝削並建立穩定的社會基礎

因此台灣的「耕者有其田」常被視為近代最成功的洛克式財產正義政策。

為什麼這些例子都指向：需要「有限政府」

洛克認為，人們建立政府的目的，是避免：
	1.	土地被剝奪
	2.	自由被侵犯
	3.	生命被威脅

政府的存在，就是要保障而非剝奪這三項自然權。
若政府反而成為壓迫來源，人民有權反抗。

南非證明了：政府若與特權結盟，會變成剝奪者。
台灣證明了：政府若履行職責，可恢復自然權的正義秩序。

Lori 的見解

在洛克的框架下，土地分配的起點最能反映一個社會的正義結構。南非的問題根源在於：殖民者用「法律」定義何謂所有權，用「武力」決定誰能擁有土地，直接違背了洛克的兩大前提。相反，台灣的耕者有其田透過把土地還給真正耕作的人，實現了勞動帶來所有權的原則。這讓我更理解洛克為何強調需要有限政府——因為只有人民共同同意的政府，才不會剝奪人的生命、自由和財產。土地制度的公平與否，往往決定了整個政治體制會走向自由還是專制。
流亡海外的委瑞內拉的人民也充份表達出:有限政府若沒有在人民共同同意下建立的政府，人民只是逃離家園的難民。我們應該探討為什麼在人民沒有共同同意下的政府，會導致國家的衰敗及貪污並將國家變成了家族企業呢? 而委瑞內拉的國家制度系統卻無法牽制領導人權利擴大的界限?
