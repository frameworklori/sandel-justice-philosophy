# Justice — Lecture 3 Summary + Your Commentary

I. Core Concept of Lecture 3（English）

“Do we own ourselves?” — Libertarianism vs. Social Obligations

In Lecture 3 of Michael Sandel’s Justice, the central debate is whether individuals
have absolute ownership over themselves—and therefore over their labor, talents,
and the wealth derived from them.

Libertarians (such as Robert Nozick) argue:
	•	If you own your body,
	•	then you own your labor,
	•	therefore you own the income made from it.

Thus, taxation for redistribution is seen as a violation of self-ownership.

Sandel challenges this idea by asking three key questions:
	1.	Are talents truly “self-made,” or are they morally arbitrary?
	2.	Can wealth exist without the social structures that support its creation?
	3.	Does individual freedom require collective obligations to maintain a stable society?

These challenges open the door to the idea that wealth and freedom are not purely individual,
but deeply relational.

II. 第三講核心概念（中文）

「人是否完全擁有自己？」—— 自我所有權、稅制、社會義務的辯論

在 Michael Sandel 的《正義》第三堂課中，主題聚焦在：

個人是否對自己、對自身能力與所創造的財富擁有「絕對所有權」。

自由至上主義者（如 Nozick）主張：
	•	你擁有你的身體，
	•	因此你擁有你的勞動，
	•	因此你擁有勞動產生的財富。

所以他們認為：
任何重新分配式的課稅，本質上都侵犯了個人的自我所有權。

但 Sandel 提出質疑：
	1.	天賦是否真的是「自我創造」？還是純粹運氣？
	2.	若沒有社會制度，財富能否被創造？
	3.	個體自由本身是否需要社會穩定與義務來維持？

這些提問讓學生開始反思：
個人自由與財富並不是完全屬於個體的，而是依賴整個社會互動而成立。

III. LORI View (English)

My Original Commentary (clean, polished, GitHub-ready)

Personal wealth can never belong entirely to the individual,
for its creation depends not only on one’s own effort,
but also on the stability, institutions, and emotional climate of the society that surrounds it.
In nations with visible ethnic or cultural divisions,
the concentration of wealth in the hands of a recognizable minority
often generates resentment, social tension, and, at times, open violence.
For this reason, the wealthy bear obligations that extend beyond moral charity:
their contributions to the broader community are a practical necessity
for preserving social cohesion, reducing intergroup hostility,
and ultimately protecting their own safety and long-term prosperity.”

IV. LORI的觀點（中文）

（精準、正式、可直接放入 GitHub）

個人的財富從來不可能100%屬於個人，
因為財富的形成不只是來自個人的努力，
更依賴周遭社會的穩定、制度的運作，以及集體的情緒氛圍。

在具有明顯族群或文化分歧的國家中，
當財富集中在「可辨識的少數族群」手上時，
往往會引發怨恨、社會緊張，甚至在某些情況下造成公開暴力。

因此，富人的責任遠超過道德層面的慈善：
回饋社會是一種實際必要，
是維持社會凝聚力、減少族群敵意，
並最終保護自身安全與長期繁榮的關鍵。
